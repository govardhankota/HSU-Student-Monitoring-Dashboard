HSU Student Monitoring Dashboard

A unified analytics platform that combines academic, financial, housing, and engagement data to support early identification of at-risk students and data-informed advising.

Executive Summary

The HSU Student Monitoring Dashboard is an integrated, data-driven solution designed to support universities in understanding and improving student success. Across higher education, institutions struggle with fragmented data spread across academic, housing, financial, and engagement systems, making it difficult for advisors to identify students who may be at risk. This project addresses that challenge by creating a unified platform that consolidates all student information into an interactive, analytics-focused dashboard.

Using a structured ETL pipeline in Power Query and a star-schema data model, the system merges academic performance metrics, financial standing, housing indicators, and co-curricular engagement. These datasets are transformed into a FactStudent table and multiple supporting dimensions, enabling deep analysis and drill-down capabilities. A set of DAX-based KPIs—including risk score, attendance trends, academic flags, engagement levels, and financial compliance—forms the core of the analytics engine.

The final Power BI dashboard presents two main reporting views: a Students Overview Page that summarizes risk levels across the campus, and a Student Details Page offering a 360-degree profile for each individual student. Together, these insights support academic advisors, program coordinators, and student support staff in making data-informed decisions and strengthening institutional retention strategies.

Problem Context & Stakeholders

Universities generate large volumes of student data across multiple departments, but these systems often operate in isolation. Advisors receive academic updates separately from financial alerts, housing concerns, or engagement data. This fragmented approach makes it difficult to identify students who may be struggling, resulting in delayed interventions and potential student dropouts.

The Problem

  • Student data is siloed across academic, financial, housing, and engagement systems.
  • Advisors lack a unified view of student progress and challenges.
  • Manual reporting leads to delays, inconsistency, and missed signals.
  • Institutions need early-warning indicators to improve retention.

Stakeholders

  • Students – Receive timely, targeted support and reduced academic risk.
  • Academic Advisors – Need a 360° view of each student’s situation.
  • Student Success Offices – Monitor risk levels and intervention outcomes.
  • Financial Services – Track balances, aid eligibility, and compliance.
  • Housing Staff – Understand conflicts, housing stability, and residency engagement.
  • University Leadership – Use aggregated insights to guide policy and resource allocation.

Research & Insights

Research in higher education analytics highlights that early identification of at-risk students is critical for improving retention and completion rates. Studies show that monitoring GPA, attendance, and engagement patterns can significantly improve the effectiveness of academic advising and support programs.

The literature also emphasizes that single-domain indicators are insufficient. Combining academic performance, financial stress, housing stability, and engagement provides a more accurate picture of student success. Institutions increasingly rely on predictive analytics and dashboards to operationalize this multi-dimensional view.

These findings informed the design of the HSU Student Monitoring Dashboard in several ways: the model includes features from all four domains, calculates a composite risk score, and allows advisors to drill down into detailed student histories. The solution aligns with practices used in commercial student-success platforms while remaining adaptable to HSU’s specific needs and data environment.

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is a unified Power BI–based analytics platform that consolidates student data from Academic, Finance, Housing, and Engagement systems into an integrated monitoring dashboard. Advisors and student support staff can quickly scan campus-wide trends and then drill into individual student profiles when risk indicators appear.

Key Features

  • 360° student profiles combining academic, financial, housing, and engagement metrics.
  • A DAX-based composite risk score and risk banding (low, medium, high).
  • Early-warning indicators for GPA drops, attendance issues, or financial non-compliance.
  • Two interactive dashboards: Students Overview and Student Detail.
  • Row-level security (RLS) to restrict access to students assigned to specific advisors.
  • Filterable views by term, program, risk level, and engagement status.

This solution modernizes advising workflows by replacing manual reports with dynamic, visually rich dashboards that highlight where attention is needed most.

Project Charter

Design and implement a unified student success dashboard that enables HSU advisors and administrators to monitor risk, support decision-making, and improve retention outcomes.

Architecture & Data Design

Enterprise Architecture

The HSU Student Monitoring Dashboard uses a layered architecture that separates data ingestion, transformation, storage, analytics, and presentation. Data from Academic, Finance, Housing, and Engagement systems flows through an integration layer into a central data model, which is then surfaced through interactive Power BI dashboards.

  • Source Systems: Academic, Finance, Housing, Engagement.
  • Integration Layer: Power Query for cleaning, transforming, and engineering features.
  • Data Layer: FactStudent table plus DimAcademic, DimFinancial, DimHousing, and DimEngagement.
  • Analytics Layer: DAX KPIs, composite risk score, and row-level security logic.
  • Presentation Layer: Students Overview and Student Detail Power BI pages.

Entity–Relationship (ER) Model

The ER model organizes student data across four domains. The Academic table anchors the model via student_id, while Finance, Engagement, and Housing tables reference it through foreign keys. This structure reduces redundancy and supports flexible analysis in Power BI.

  • Academic Department: GPA, attendance, probation, course load, and risk metrics.
  • Finance: Fees paid, outstanding balance, financial aid, and scholarship eligibility.
  • Engagement: Club memberships, event attendance, volunteer service, tutoring sessions.
  • Housing: Conflicts, housing stability, distance from campus, residence events.

Data Flow Explanation

  1. Raw data is exported or queried from departmental systems.
  2. Power Query cleans, normalizes, and joins these sources using student_id.
  3. Cleaned tables are loaded into the FactStudent and dimension tables.
  4. DAX measures compute KPIs, risk scores, and banding classifications.
  5. Power BI dashboards visualize the results, filtered by role and access level.

The Students Overview Page highlights campus-wide trends, such as the distribution of students across risk bands, average GPA by program, and the relationship between engagement and academic outcomes. Filters allow users to slice results by term, major, housing status, or financial standing.

The Student Detail Page provides a 360° profile of a single student. Advisors can review academic history, engagement patterns, financial indicators, and housing context in one place, along with the underlying risk score breakdown. This view supports targeted outreach and tailored intervention planning.

Ethical & Social Considerations

Because the dashboard handles sensitive student information, ethical and social considerations are central to its design. The system is intended to support students, not to label or penalize them.

  • Privacy & FERPA: Access to identifiable data should be limited to authorized staff and managed through role-based security.
  • Bias & Fairness: Risk scores are based on academic and behavioral indicators, not demographic attributes, to reduce the potential for bias.
  • Transparency: Advisors should understand which factors contribute to a student’s risk score and be able to explain these to the student.
  • Responsible Use: Insights are meant to guide supportive interventions, such as tutoring, financial counseling, or advising meetings.

Lessons Learned & Team Reflection

Building the HSU Student Monitoring Dashboard strengthened our ability to translate a real higher-education challenge into a practical analytics solution. We learned how important it is to define consistent keys, design a coherent data model, and validate metrics against realistic scenarios before creating visualizations.

Technically, the project deepened our skills in Power Query, data modeling, DAX, and dashboard storytelling. We also gained experience balancing detail and usability—deciding which metrics belong in an overview versus a drill-through page, and how to present risk in a way that is actionable rather than overwhelming.

As a team, we improved our collaboration, documentation, and version control practices. The project provided a valuable preview of how data-driven tools can support student success initiatives in real institutions and helped us develop a portfolio-ready artifact that reflects our interests in analytics and education technology.
